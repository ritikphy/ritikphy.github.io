About

A little more about me

I first became interested in physics through a fascination with symmetry — the idea that the deepest laws of nature are often the most tightly constrained ones. That interest has carried me from introductory mechanics through to the stranger territory of quantum theory, where the rules of everyday intuition stop applying and have to be rebuilt from first principles.

Alongside coursework and research, I care about making physics accessible to people outside it. I've enjoyed tutoring first-year students, helping run a departmental study group, and writing occasional explainers for a general audience (see the Blog section below).

Research

Research interests

My research sits within quantum information theory and the foundations of quantum mechanics. I am particularly interested in how entanglement and symmetry constrain the information available to different observers, and more broadly I am motivated by the search for the simplest set of principles from which the strange predictions of quantum theory follow.

Quantum Information

Entanglement, quantum channels, and the resource-theoretic view of what quantum systems can and cannot do.

Quantum Foundations

The measurement problem, interpretations of quantum theory, and what the formalism does — and doesn't — tell us about reality.

Open Quantum Systems

Decoherence, noise, and how familiar classical behaviour emerges from an underlying quantum world.

Symmetries & Subsystems

How symmetry principles constrain physical theories, and how subsystems are defined relative to an observer's reference frame.
